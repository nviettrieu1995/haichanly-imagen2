# Pham-Tran Canonical Nalas Reference

Approved file:
`pham_tran_canonical.png`

Use this image as the canonical mortal Earth / pham-tran identity for Cha Nalas Nalanda:
- Vietnamese middle-aged father-teacher
- rounded-square gentle face, thin glasses, solid grounded body
- modest grey-brown cotton clothing
- calm, learned, compassionate teacher energy
- modern clean Vietnamese father-teacher identity, with setting changing by story phase

Timeline rule:
- Before wisdom returns / before formal teaching: poor present-day Vietnamese countryside/village life around 2014-2019. Use low modest houses, quiet yard or lane, sparse lights, painted or worn plaster/brick walls, cement/tile floors, electric bulb or fluorescent tube light, fan, simple bed/table/chairs, notebooks, tea or coffee cups, and clean everyday clothes. Nalas remains modern, clean, and always wearing thin glasses.
- After Nalas starts teaching / gathers disciples: cleaner, brighter, better-supported Vietnamese learning rooms with proper desks/chairs, books, shelves, notebooks, tea or coffee, and organized students.
- Covid/post-Covid and later teaching: clearly modern 2020-2026 Vietnamese city life, cleaner office-classrooms with LED/tube lights, proper desks/chairs, magnetic whiteboard, marker pens, shelves, notebooks, tea or coffee, and organized students.
- Avoid the wrong rural look: no nha tranh vach dat, clay/mud walls, thatched huts, oil lamps, dirt floors, low floor mats, patched peasant clothing, rice fields, fishing boats, muddy canals, riverside villages, rural lotus ponds, river-delta nostalgia, floating markets, rural water villages, wooden canal bridges, or mien que song nuoc. Early rural scenes are poor present-day home/yard/lane scenes, not scenic countryside-water nostalgia. After the teaching phase begins, if water is unavoidable, use an urban canal, city lake, or riverfront promenade with concrete paths, railings, streetlights, buildings, and traffic/city context.

Do not use this reference for true heaven/celestial versions except when the sleeping or waking mortal body is visible.
