Use this image as the canonical mortal Earth / pham-tran identity for Cha Nalas Nalanda:
- Vietnamese middle-aged father-teacher
- rounded-square gentle face, thin glasses, solid grounded body
- modest grey-brown cotton clothing
- calm, learned, compassionate teacher energy
- modest modern Vietnamese urban teaching warmth for Earth chapters

Timeline rule:
- Early pham-tran: ordinary modern urban Vietnam around 2014-2019, modest but clean rooms in a normal city district, painted walls, tiled floors, electric lights, fan, simple desk/table, chairs, notebooks, tea or coffee cups, and clean everyday clothes.
- Covid/post-Covid and later teaching: clearly modern 2020-2026 Vietnamese city life, cleaner and brighter classrooms or office-classrooms with LED/tube lights, proper desks/chairs, magnetic whiteboard, marker pens, shelves, notebooks, tea or coffee, and organized students.
- Avoid the old rural look for Earth scenes: no nha tranh vach dat, clay/mud walls, thatched huts, oil lamps, dirt floors, low floor mats, patched peasant clothing, rice fields, fishing boats, muddy canals, riverside villages, rural lotus ponds, river-delta nostalgia, floating markets, rural water villages, wooden canal bridges, or mien que song nuoc. Pham-tran scenes should read as ordinary modern Vietnamese city districts, classrooms, homes, offices, and paved streets from 2014 onward, visually close to 2020-2026. If the excerpt is generic, choose a city home, apartment room, ward street, shopfront, office-classroom, or training room instead of a scenic landscape. If water is unavoidable, use an urban canal, city lake, or riverfront promenade with concrete paths, railings, streetlights, buildings, and traffic/city context.

Do not use this reference for true heaven/celestial versions except when the sleeping or waking mortal body is visible.
